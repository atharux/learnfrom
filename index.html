Most teams build things users don't use.

Internal tools get abandoned. Dashboards nobody reads. Processes that create more friction than they solve. The reason is almost always the same — nobody ran the research, mapped the journey, or measured what "better" actually looks like.

UX and Six Sigma solve exactly this problem. Together, they give you a system: research what users actually need, design around it, and measure whether it worked.

Course Curriculum

8 modules · Self-paced · Practical exercises included

01

Introduction to UX

What UX is (and isn't), UX roles in real teams, how UX fits into Agile workflows, and why companies with strong design outperform competitors by 2x.

02

User Research + Personas

Research mindset, interview frameworks, qualitative vs. quantitative methods, building realistic personas from real data, and user flow diagrams.

03

Usability Principles

Don Norman's core principles — mapping, affordance, feedback, signifiers, constraints, discoverability — applied to real product examples.

04

UX Analysis

Heuristic analysis, cognitive load reduction, journey mapping, and behavior analysis. Includes a full walkthrough exercise with a real product.

05

Wireframing + Prototyping

From low-fidelity sketches to clickable prototypes. Using the Octalysis framework to design onboarding, scaffolding, and end-game user experiences.

06

Black Hat vs. White Hat Design

Gamification ethics, the 8 Core Motivations, and how to design for long-term engagement without exploiting users.

07

Form Design

Why forms fail, friction reduction strategies, win states, real-time feedback patterns, and how to audit any form in under 30 minutes.

08

Lean Six Sigma for UX

DMAIC applied to UX design. How to define, measure, and prove the improvement from your design work using real process data. Includes a complete dashboard redesign case study.
